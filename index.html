Coursework Highlights

  • 18.600 - Introduction to Probability and Random Variables, Fall 2019

    Covered the theory of random variables, including properties of continuous and discrete distrubutions, and applications to real-world problems.

  • 1.063 - Introduction to Fluids and Diseases, Spring 2020

Extra-Curricular and Project Highlights

  • Undergraduate Association Committee on COVID-19, Fall 2020 - Spring 2021

    Worked with students, student leaders, and administrators to identify and solve student body problems that arose in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. First, I led a team that focused on understanding MIT public health policy to enhance student decision-making capabilities, and then I co-led a team that advocated for expansion of the number and variety of on-campus spaces that were available for students to work in.

  • Quantifying Key Input Uncertainties in the Global Change Analysis Model (GCAM), Present

    Using the EPPA climate model, I study which input factors have the largest impact on the climatologically important output factors (e.g. carbon emissions). A first round of results is has been published under "Representing Socio-Economic Uncertainty in Human System Models".

  • Dynamics of Homologous Recombination in S. Pombe, June 2017 - August 2018

    Worked in the lab of Megan King in the Boyer Center for Molecular Medicine (Yale School of Medicine) under graduate student (now Dr.) Amanda Vines and studied the mechanism of homologous recombination (a DNA repair technique) in yeast. The work in the lab led to a paper on our results. Image credit: MicrobeWiki
